who we are

The Push That Started It All

Back in 1997, I had a comfortable consulting career ahead of me. But something kept bothering me. Business owners would say:

"Export is only for big companies." "We don't know how to find international buyers." "Documentation looks too complicated."

The problem wasn't capability. The problem was clarity and confidence.

So I started Global Network Institute — not as a training company, but as a bridge. A bridge between Indian businesses and global markets.

The goal was simple: Make export accessible. Then make it successful.

our journey

The Years of Discovery

The early years were filled with travel, trade fairs, buyer meetings, and plenty of learning.

Yes, mistakes happened. Wrong exhibitions. Unverified buyers. Outdated documentation methods.

But every mistake refined our system. I learned something powerful:

Export isn't just paperwork. It's relationships, trust, positioning, and strategy.

That realization shaped everything we teach today.

milestone

The Breakthrough Moment

In 2005, one of our participants — a small handicraft manufacturer — closed a $500,000 order with a European buyer we connected him with.

He called me in tears. "Jagat bhai, this one order is bigger than my last five years of business."

That day changed everything. We weren't just teaching export. We were transforming businesses and building confidence.

From that moment, I made one promise: Every participant deserves real opportunities — not just a certificate.

insights

What I've Learned

If there's one thing international trade has taught me, it's this: You cannot scale exports alone.

Global markets are different. Cultures are different. Payment systems are different. Buyer expectations are different.

That's why we don't just train. We mentor. We guide. We connect.
